The Smart Move

Fly via CBX — Cross Border Xpress

Instead of flying out of a US airport, you drive to the Cross Border Xpress bridge in Otay Mesa — a dedicated pedestrian sky bridge that connects directly into the Tijuana airport terminal. Walk across, check in, fly. No US airport markups.

San Diego/Tijuana border. CBX is open 24/7 with paid parking on the US side.

Where We Stay

Junior Suite

One elongated suite — king bed at one end, full sofa sitting area at the other, all connected as a single open room. Warm amber walls, four-poster bed, ceiling fan, and sliding glass doors to a private terrace with a hammock. The suite's natural length means the sitting area at the far end is the obvious spot for a kid's crib — physically away from the sleeping bed but still in the same room.

Premium Level rooms sit in a quieter section of the Palace complex — closer to the private lounge, further from the main pool bar noise.

Honest Assessment

Is Premium Level Worth It?

Reviews are split. The biggest concrete benefits for a family with small kids:

  • ✓  Restaurant priority matters — specialty à la carte books out fast in peak season. Getting two reservations at check-in vs. one is a real advantage.
  • ✓  Seated check-in with drinks served is genuinely nice after a long travel day with two kids.
  • ✓  Lounge breakfast with the kids is low-key vs. the main buffet rush.

Caveat: Several guests note the "upgraded spirits" claim is inconsistent — the main bar also pours well. The primary payoff for this trip is the restaurant priority and the quieter room location.

A Few Things to Know Going In

  • ·  À la carte restaurant booking can be competitive. Priority from Premium Level helps, but flexibility on dinner times (8–9:30pm slots are easier to get than 6pm) makes a real difference.
  • ·  Spring break (March) draws louder crowds and loud pool music. October is quieter — resort atmosphere will be calmer and more family-appropriate.
  • ·  A small number of guests note an inconsistency in service quality. The overall consensus from recent reviews leans strongly positive, but individual staff experiences can vary.

What to Expect

Warm, Sunny, with Afternoon Showers

October is still technically rainy season, but Riviera Maya's pattern is predictable: strong sunshine most of the day, followed by a brief afternoon or evening shower that rarely lasts more than a few hours. Humidity runs around 78%. Water temperature is at its peak — bath-warm and beautiful. Ocean conditions at the Palace's reef-protected beach remain calm and gentle throughout.

Hurricane Season Note

Low Risk, Good Timing

October is technically within hurricane season (June–October), but actual landfall risk for any specific week is very small. Peak hurricane activity is August–September; by mid-October statistical risk drops further. Travel insurance is always worth carrying. October's big upside: significantly lower hotel prices and lighter crowds than winter peak season (December–March).
